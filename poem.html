The Fig Tree of Futures

I saw my life branching outward,
like a green fig tree trembling in possibility.
From every tip, a future ripened—
fat, purple, glowing with promise.

One fig held a home, warm and bright, with laughter spilling from its windows.
Another whispered of becoming a poet.
Another shimmered—professor, thinker, voice in crowded halls.

The Many Figs

Each future called to me:

  • A life of love and children
  • A famous poet shaping words
  • A brilliant professor of ideas
  • An editor with vision and fire

And beyond them:

  1. Journeys through Europe, Africa, and South America
  2. Lovers with strange and beautiful names
  3. Victories—an Olympic champion

The Hunger of Choice

I wanted all of them.
Every single fig, every shining path.
But choosing one meant losing the rest.

So I stayed still,
caught in the branches,
afraid to reach.

The Falling

Time did not wait.
The figs began to wrinkle,
softness fading into shadow.

One by one,
they fell.
Quiet, heavy, final.

Reflection

And I remained,
hungry among abundance,
watching possibility turn to memory.
